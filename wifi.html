Build Your Own WiFi Hacking Tool with the Nugget!

By: Alex Lynd
@alexlynd

Abstract:

In the first part of this workshop, attendees are familiarized with soldering techniques & tools, and are guided through assembling a ""Nugget"" kit, which comprises a cat-shaped PCB, screen, microcontroller, and a 3D printed enclosure.

The second half of the workshop introduces WiFi hacking concepts and familiarizes beginners with common techniques, attack vectors, and real-world scenarios. Attendees will get a cursory introduction to the WiFi protocol & how attacks work - and will build a simple CircuitPython-based network defense tool on their Nugget to detect WiFi attacks.  

As a bonus / if it's permitted, beginners will also be introduced to WiFi hacking from an offensive perspective and learn to craft their own attacks using a custom Deauther firmware on their WiFi Nuggets, and learn how malicious actors can use techniques like KARMA to take advantage of WiFi flaws.

This workshop is beginner friendly! They will be guided through soldering, coding, and also WiFi hacking. It helps to have some technical knowledge of any of these subjects.
